DOCUMENT 01 / ENTRY SIGNAL / 29 JUNE 2017

GP Referral

The document that fixed the problem the hospital accepted: a foot-centred gait failure requiring neurological explanation.

PUBLIC GUIDE

This is the entry document. It defines what the receiving institution was being asked to explain.

The referral matters because it fixes the case before the hospital has had any opportunity to translate it into its own preferred diagnostic language. It is the cleanest pre-hospital statement of the problem.

The patient was not referred as a settled Parkinson's case. He was referred because gait, movement initiation, stiffness, tone, employment capacity, and significant foot pathology had converged into an urgent neurological problem. The feet were not a footnote to that problem. They were part of the reason the problem existed in the form it did.

The referral preserved diagnostic openness while anchoring the problem in the feet.

This distinction controls the rest of the archive. The hospital was not simply being invited to place a label on a collection of signs. It was being asked to explain why this person could not move normally, given the visible and reported foot-centred impairment. Any later diagnostic frame therefore had to remain answerable to that initiating signal.

A lawful sequence required one of three outcomes. The feet had to be explained neurologically. Or they had to be empirically excluded as the governing driver. Or they had to be explicitly superseded by a better-tested explanation that accounted for why the referral-defined problem no longer controlled the case.

What could not safely happen was this: the signs could be abstracted into Parkinsonian language while the original foot-centred problem was left unresolved and then displaced into another pathway.

Once accepted, the referral became a constraint on closure.

This is why the referral is not background. It is an anchor. It prevents the later record from pretending that the feet were an incidental complaint, a later reinterpretation, or a peripheral inconvenience. The feet were already there at the gate.

DOCUMENT FUNCTION

The GP referral defines the accepted scope.

It preserves openness.

It makes the feet governing.
